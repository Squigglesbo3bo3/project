About the disease

What is Pneumonia?

Pneumonia is a lung infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, causing them to fill with fluid or pus. It ranges from mild to life-threatening and is a leading cause of death, especially in children under 5 and adults over 65.

Chest X-rays remain the gold standard for diagnosis. Our AI model is trained to detect the characteristic infiltrates and consolidations that appear on infected lungs.

Common symptoms
High fever & chills
Productive cough
Shortness of breath
Sharp chest pain
Fatigue & weakness
Loss of appetite

Typical progression

1
Infection onset
Bacteria, viruses, or fungi enter lung tissue. Mild symptoms start within 24–48 hours.
2
Consolidation phase
Air sacs fill with fluid and immune cells. Fever peaks, oxygen levels may drop significantly.
3
Treatment & recovery
With antibiotics or antivirals, most patients recover within 1–3 weeks. Follow-up X-ray recommended.
